Northern Cardinal


Size

The Northern Cardinal is a fairly large, long-tailed songbird with a short, very thick bill and a prominent crest. Cardinals often sit with a hunched-over posture and with the tail pointed straight down.

Nesting

Northern Cardinals lay their eggs anywhere between February and September. Cardinals lay 3 to 4 eggs, sometimes 2 to 5. The eggs are whitish to pale bluish or greenish white, marked with brown, purple, and gray spots. The female incubates for 12 to 13 days.

Male cardinals are a bright red all over, with a reddish bill and black face immediately around the bill. Females are pale brown overall with warm reddish tints in the wings, tail, and crest. They have the same black face and red-orange bill. Juveniles are similar to females, but have a gray to black bill.

Diet

Mostly seeds, insects, berries. Feeds on many insects, including beetles, grasshoppers, caterpillars, ants, flies, spiders, centipedes, and snails. Most of diet is vegetable matter, including seeds of weeds and grasses, waste grain, leaf buds, flowers, and many berries and wild fruits.
