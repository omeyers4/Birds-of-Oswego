Eastern Bluebird


Size

The Eastern Bluebird is a small thrush with a big rounded head, large eyes, and plump body. The wings are long, but the tail and legs are fairly short. The bill is short and straight.

Nesting

Egg-laying begins during February and March in the warmer southern states and March and April in the northern and colder states. Bluebirds typically lay 4 to 5 eggs, though in some cases, they can have clutch sizes of up to 7 eggs. The color of the Bluebird egg is normally blue. But rarely some females lay white eggs or pink eggs.

The Male Eastern Bluebirds are a vivid, deep blue above and rusty or brick-red on the throat and breast. Females are grayish above with bluish wings and tail, and a subdued orange-brown breast. Juveniles have spots on their back and chest with variable amounts of blue in the wings and tail.

Diet

Feeds on a wide variety of insects, including crickets, grasshoppers, beetles, spiders, earthworms, snails, rarely small lizards or tree frogs. Also eats many berries, especially in winter.

Habitat

Eastern Bluebirds live in meadows and openings surrounded by trees that offer suitable nest holes. With the increase in nest boxes and bluebird trails, bluebirds are now a common sight along roads, field edges, golf courses, and other open areas. During the winter Bluebirds migrate to southeastern U.S. or Mexico. Some fly as far as 2,000 miles between western Manitoba and Texas.
